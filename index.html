Word

Paste this into Copilot in Word.

Based on everything you can see across my Microsoft 365 environment — including my emails, Teams chats, meeting transcripts, documents, and the type of work I do — analyse how I typically create and use documents in Word.

Identify the most common types of documents I work on, such as reports, proposals, summaries, training materials, communications, or formal documents. Look for patterns in the requests I receive and the way I structure my content.

Then generate a set of high-quality, practical Copilot prompts I can use in Microsoft Word to speed up and improve my work.

Important:
- Your output must be a list of copy-and-paste ready prompts only
- Each prompt must be fully written so I can use it immediately in Copilot in Word without editing
- Do not include explanations unless they are short and directly tied to the prompt
- Make each prompt specific, realistic, and grounded in the kind of work I actually do

For each prompt:
- Write it as if I am speaking to Copilot
- Include enough context so the prompt works on its own
- Make it clear what inputs Copilot should use (for example: "based on this document", "using the following notes", "from this meeting transcript")

Include a variety of prompt types, such as:
- drafting documents from scratch
- summarising existing content
- rewriting or improving tone and clarity
- structuring long documents
- generating executive summaries
- turning notes or transcripts into polished documents

Also include a few more advanced prompts that combine Word with other M365 content, such as:
- turning an email thread into a formal document
- converting a Teams meeting transcript into a report
- combining multiple documents into a single cohesive output

Focus on prompts that will save me time, improve quality, and reflect how I actually work day-to-day.

Email

Paste this into Microsoft 365 Copilot or Copilot in Outlook — it will ask you questions first.

I want to build a personalised inbox prioritisation framework that I can reuse when asking Copilot to triage my emails. Ask me a series of questions (one at a time) to understand how I decide what is important vs not important in my inbox.

Your questions should help uncover:
- What types of emails I consider high priority (e.g. manager, customers, specific projects)
- Time sensitivity (what feels urgent vs can wait)
- Business impact (what actually moves work forward vs noise)
- Patterns I notice (senders, keywords, topics, meeting follow-ups, approvals, etc.)
- What I tend to ignore or deprioritise
- Any exceptions or edge cases
- How I currently feel about my inbox (overwhelmed, in control, reactive, etc.)
- What "a good inbox day" looks like for me

Once you've gathered enough information, synthesise my responses into a clear, structured "Inbox Prioritisation Guide" that includes:
- My prioritisation principles (in plain language)
- A tiered system (e.g. High / Medium / Low priority with rules for each)
- Specific signals to look for (people, keywords, deadlines, actions required)
- What to ignore or batch
- Any personal preferences or working style notes

Then create a final section called: "Prompt I can use with Copilot"

In that section, write a reusable prompt that uses my framework to ask Copilot to prioritise my inbox based on my preferences. Make the output practical, concise, and something I could actually reference day-to-day.
